Developer Week

It was Developer Week at the Oakland Convention Center the other week. For most of us, it was our first foray into the world of tech conferences with lots of swag and people talking about their products. My good buddy Paul and I sat in on this talk. Good times. And LOTS of free stuff!

We've been working with Django for the past week. It's a Python-based web framework that allows you to get a server up and running. The first few assignments utilizing Django had us build a simple clock, a random word generator, and a small game where a Ninja goes around and collects gold from a farm, a cave, a house and a casino. Sometimes the ninja loses gold from the casino.

Python

This week we started working with Python. Python is an interpreted, high-level, general-purpose language created by Guido van Rossum in 1991. Python's design philosophy emphasizes code readability. It definitely uses a significant amount of whitespace.

So far I am enjoying it.

  • Explicit is better than implicit

Foster City

I'm from a small suburb of San Francisco along the peninsula called Foster City. The name comes from T. Jack Foster, a developer and real-estate magnate who owned much of the area on the east edge of San Mateo. This is a photo of the San Mateo Bridge looking east toward Hayward.

Lake Tahoe

One of the perks of living in the Bay Area is it's proximity to world-class ski and snowboarding resorts. Lake Tahoe is one of the most beautiful places where I like to spend time snowboarding and enjoying the great outdoors. I recently purchased a Lib Tech snowboard and have been taking the Bay Area Ski Bus to NorthStar, Kirkwood and Heavenly.

Mariners Point Golf Complex

One of my favorite things in life is playing golf. The Mariners Point Golf Complex in Foster City is situated on the Bay with gorgeous views of the San Mateo Bridge, the San Francisco city skyline, and airplanes making their descent into SFO. The training center features a 9-hole short course, a 64-stall driving range with Toptracer technology (powered by TopGolf!), practice greens and bunkers.
